Skin Polishing

> Skin lightening is a popular cosmetic procedure that helps individuals achieve their desired level of glow. Whitening dark spots or brightening the entire complexion can give you an enviable radiance without any major change to your naturally gorgeous look.

> Common applications include covering up dark spots or blurring the appearance of birthmarks (melasma).

Pigmentation Treatment

> Large patches of discoloration, known as pigmentation markings, may appear on the face. This is because the skin pigment melanin accumulates and causes this condition. It’s a genetic predisposition that might cause abnormalities in skin tone or pigmentation in other tissues.

> Freckles, age spots, flat pigmented birthmarks, and melasma result from excessive melanin production. Sunspots and lentigines are other names for age spots. Melasma, sometimes called the pregnancy mask or dark spots on the face, is another name for freckles.

> Unwanted pigmentation marks are no longer a source of dread! There is now an array of laser treatments and specialized Skin Pigmentation Treatments available to vanquish those spots easily.

Fillers Treatment

> Fillers are a type of cosmetic treatment used to enhance facial features by filling in wrinkles, fine lines, and other areas of the face that, have lost volume or elasticity. The most common types of fillers are hyaluronic acid fillers and collagen-stimulating fillers.

> Hyaluronic acid fillers are composed of a substance, that is naturally found in the body, and they are used to fill in wrinkles and add volume to areas such as the lips and cheeks. Collagen-stimulating fillers, on the other hand, work by stimulating the body’s natural collagen production, which can help to improve skin elasticity and reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles over time.

> Filler treatments are typically performed by a licensed and trained medical professional, such as a dermatologist or plastic surgeon. During the procedure, the filler is injected into the targeted areas of the face using a fine needle, and the process typically takes about 30 minutes to complete. The results of fillers can last anywhere from several months to a year or more, depending on the type of filler used and the individual’s skin type and lifestyle factors. It is important to note that like any medical treatment there are risks associated with fillers, and individuals should discuss the potential risks and benefits with their healthcare provider before undergoing the procedure.
